Privacy by design, verifiable by default

How Blockaid handles data when securing your transactions, users, and onchain assets

Blockaid is built to produce a security verdict from the absolute minimum data required — and, in its recommended configuration, that data is public onchain information, not your customers' personal records. This brief explains exactly what Blockaid receives, what it never receives, and the controls that keep re-identification of your users in your hands.

What Blockaid receives

Blockaid's APIs operate on the entity being scanned (a transaction, address, token, or dApp) plus an optional metadata block. Every field below other than the scanned onchain entity is either a non-personal transaction attribute or optional metadata that you control and can omit or pseudonymize.

What Blockaid never receives

In the recommended configuration, Blockaid does not receive, request, or require:

  • Names, emails, phone or postal addresses
  • Government ID, passport, or KYC/AML documents
  • Date of birth, nationality, or demographics
  • Account numbers, credentials, or session tokens
  • Balances, holdings, or portfolio history
  • The customer's source / sending wallet

Why onchain data isn't customer PII

A blockchain address is a public, pseudonymous onchain identifier — not an identifier of your customer. Blockaid receives the entity being screened, never the sender's identity, and does not enrich it with any identity data. Because Blockaid never receives who your customer is, it cannot re-identify them. Querying Blockaid before broadcast exposes nothing that isn't already about to become public on the ledger.

How the data flows

your risk engine  ──( onchain entity + optional context )──▶  Blockaid API
your risk engine  ◀──────────( security verdict )──────────  Blockaid API

A synchronous request / response model. Blockaid does not initiate connections back into your systems, does not require access to any of your databases, and returns only a verdict plus the matched indicators. Your own rules decide what happens next.

You stay the controller

Any correlation token is chosen and controlled by you and can be fully pseudonymized — the means of re-identification never leaves your environment.

Purpose limitation

Data submitted in a scan is processed for the sole purpose of returning that security verdict.

Secure transport

All API traffic is over TLS (HTTPS) and authenticated with a tenant-specific API key.

Compliance & contractual assurances

Data-processing roles, retention periods, sub-processors, security controls, and breach-notification terms are formalized in a Data Processing Addendum (DPA) aligned to your standard requirements.
